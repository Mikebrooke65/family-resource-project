Welcome
This document gets you set up and tells you where to start.
The short version: we are building the resource that doesn't exist — a dedicated, always-available digital platform for NZ parents whose child is self-harming. Everything you find online is written for the young person. This is written for the parent.

"When your child is self-harming, everything you find online is written for them. This is written for you."

You're coming in at the right moment. The research foundation is solid. The gap is confirmed. The first key decision we need to make together — the name — is ready for your input.

Your Reading Order — Start Here
Before we work on anything together, read these three documents in order. They are the foundation for the naming decision and everything that comes after it.

1. Parent Emotional Journey — read this first
https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Mikebrooke65/family-resource-project/refs/heads/main/docs/parent-emotional-journey.md
This is the most important document in the project. It's based on peer-reviewed research and captures exactly what is happening for a parent in the moment they discover their child is self-harming — and what they are searching for online and why.
What to take from it:

The six core psychoeducation needs — this is our content architecture; every piece of content we build sits within one of these six areas
The emotion word bank — the exact words parents use to describe their experience; these belong in our content and brand voice, not clinical language
The search behaviour analysis — what parents are typing at 11pm and what they need the moment they land on a page
The tone of voice principles — parent to parent, calm, validating before informing, hopeful

This document will also tell you a lot about why our founding story matters as a brand asset.

2. NZ Research — focus on the conclusions
https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Mikebrooke65/family-resource-project/refs/heads/main/research/nz-landscape.md
This is a comprehensive audit of every NZ organisation and digital tool in this space. You don't need to read every line — focus on:

The Gap section — the core competitive finding
Yellow Brick Road — our primary referral partner; understand who they are and why
Conclusions and Key Takeaways — the full summary at the end

The headline: we have no direct competitors. Every NZ digital mental health tool is youth-facing. Not one is parent-facing. Not one is self-harm specific. We are filling a completely unoccupied space — confirmed by exhaustive research, not assumption.

3. Brand Brief — where your input matters most
https://raw.githubusercontent.com/Mikebrooke65/family-resource-project/refs/heads/main/docs/brand-brief.md
We have a shortlist of three names and need to agree a decision together before we can register a domain and move forward.
The shortlist:

Alongside — someone is walking with you through this; warm, active, implies ongoing companionship
Not Alone — directly addresses the most documented parent emotion (isolation); names exactly what parents feel and need to hear
Still Here — emotionally powerful dual meaning; slight ambiguity worth discussing

Mike's current lean is toward Alongside, with Not Alone as a strong second. But this is genuinely a joint call — your instinct as someone who has been through this is exactly the test the name needs to pass.
The question to hold in your mind as you read: If I had found this at 11pm when I was frightened and didn't know what to do — would this name have made me feel like I was in the right place?
