### Disproportionately Affected Groups
- **Māori youth** — depressive symptoms rose from 14% to 28% (2012–2019); suicide attempts doubled to 13%
- **Rainbow/LGBTQ+ youth** — 64% have thought about suicide; 10% have attempted it
- **Girls** — depression rates consistently nearly double those of males
- **High deprivation areas** — suicide attempt rates double between deprivation tiers

## Existing NZ Resources (Audit)

| Organisation | What They Offer | Gap |
|---|---|---|
| KidsHealth NZ | General self-harm page, listening guidance | Not parent-specific, not self-harm-focused |
| Mental Health Foundation | 2025 guide for parents in mental distress | General MH, not self-harm specific |
| Yellow Brick Road | Family support, group sessions, Family Connections programme | Not self-harm specific |
| 1737 | Free 24/7 crisis line | Crisis only, not educational |
| Healthline | 0800 611 116 | Medical, not parent guidance |

**Conclusion:** No single, parent-centred resource specifically designed for the moment a parent discovers their child is self-harming. That is the gap.

## Yellow Brick Road — Key Partner

- Formerly Supporting Families NZ — 40+ years experience
- Core belief: supporting the family improves wellbeing for whole whānau
- Services: one-on-one support, group sessions, advocacy, peer support, suicidal distress support
- **Family Connections programme** — for families of those with BPD or severe emotion dysregulation (closely linked to self-harm)
- All services FREE and confidential
- Contact: **0800 732 825** | hello@yellowbrickroad.org.nz | Mon–Fri

**Action:** Meet with Yellow Brick Road early — natural primary referral partner, possible collaboration.

## Why Parents Don't Talk About It

1. **Shame/guilt** — parents feel it reflects personal failure; everyone suffers in silence
2. **Misunderstanding** — most associate self-harm with suicide attempts or attention-seeking (reality: usually emotion regulation)
3. **School caution** — contagion concerns mean schools rarely discuss it
4. **Clinical gaps** — GPs and teachers often don't know what to say either
5. **Extra taboo layer** — even as general MH conversations improve, self-harm carries additional stigma
