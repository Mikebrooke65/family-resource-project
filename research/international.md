## UK

### YoungMinds (youngminds.org.uk) - MOST RELEVANT COMPARATOR
- UK's leading youth mental health charity — strong parent focus
- Dedicated self-harm section within a full A-Z parent mental health guide
- Parents Helpline — free phone and webchat, Mon-Fri (0808 802 5544)
- Parents Lounge — specific section supporting parents respond to self-harm and have difficult conversations
- Content co-produced with parents and young people with lived experience (important credibility signal)
- PIF TICK certified — UK's quality mark for trusted health information
- Also has a professionals section with training resources — "No Harm Done" digital packs for schools
- Crisis text line (free, 24/7) separate from parent helpline
- Key learning: Structure of A-Z guide with separate parent/young person pathways works well. Parents Lounge concept is closest thing internationally to what we're building. Strong lived experience co-production model.
- Gap vs our vision: Still embedded within a general mental health charity — not self-harm specific. NZ has no equivalent at all.

### Stem4 (stem4.org.uk) - WORTH STUDYING CLOSELY
- UK charity focused specifically on teen mental health — not general MH
- Has dedicated self-harm section for both teens and parents/carers separately
- Calm Harm app — free app helping teenagers resist or manage urge to self-harm (highly regarded)
- Combined Minds app — for families supporting young people
- Education and training: digital programmes (Head Ed), interactive school conferences
- School support packs — direct school licensing model (relevant to our Phase 2)
- Key learning: The app-based approach (Calm Harm) is widely cited. School licensing model is exactly what we're planning for Phase 2. Separate parent/teen content architecture is smart.

### PAPYRUS (papyrus-uk.org)
- Focused specifically on youth suicide prevention — adjacent but not self-harm specific
- Runs HopeLineUK (crisis support for young people and those concerned about them)
- School training programmes — ASIST, safeTALK
- Key learning: Specialisation (suicide-specific) has given them strong brand recognition. Model for how a niche focus builds credibility.

## Australia

### ReachOut Parents (parents.au.reachout.com) - STRONG COMPARATOR
- Dedicated parent-facing platform — separate from the youth-facing ReachOut
- Has specific self-harm section with practical strategies
- Also offers one-on-one coaching support for parents
- School resources arm: ReachOut Schools — separate product for educators
- Key learning: The separation of parent platform from youth platform is smart UX. One-on-one coaching is a Phase 2/3 revenue opportunity we haven't listed. School product is a clear Phase 2 model.
- Gap: Australian context — not NZ specific, doesn't fill the NZ gap

### Beyond Blue (beyondblue.org.au)
- Broad mental health organisation — parenting section exists but not self-harm specific
- Signposts to ReachOut for teen/parent content rather than owning it
- Less directly relevant

## USA

### Youth Mental Health Foundation / The Horizon Plan (youthmentalhealthfoundation.org) - CLOSEST MODEL TO OURS
- Founded by a parent (Claire Sutton) based on lived experience of daughter's self-harm — direct parallel to our founding story
- Free online course (The Horizon Plan) — two modules with downloadable workbooks, video-based
- Power2Parent groups — live version of the course with peer support, individual check-ins, group discussions (paid)
- OASIS community — ongoing membership/community model
- Self-Harm Booklet — free downloadable resource
- Facebook Support Group — closed group for parents
- School products: assembly programme, Genius Workshop, School Support Pack
- Key learning: This is the closest international model to what we're building. Same founding story (lived experience), same audience (parents), same phased model (free resource -> paid courses -> community). They have done Phase 1, 2 and 3 — we can learn from their journey.
- URL: https://youthmentalhealthfoundation.org

### NAMI — Family Resources
- Large US organisation with family-focused programmes
- Family-to-Family course: free 8-session programme for family members
- Key learning: Peer-led model (family members teaching family members) is powerful and cost-effective. Worth considering for Phase 3.

### Mental Health First Aid (mentalhealthfirstaid.org)
- Structured training courses for identifying and responding to mental health issues
- Youth MHFA specifically designed for adults who work with young people
- Key learning: Certification/accreditation model for training creates credibility and a reason to pay

## School Market — What Are Schools Buying?

- Stem4 (UK): Digital programmes, school conferences, support packs — per-school or per-student licensing
- PAPYRUS (UK): ASIST and safeTALK training — per-person certification model
- ReachOut Schools (Australia): Separate school product — subscription model
- Youth Mental Health Foundation: School assembly, Genius Workshop, School Support Pack
- Mental Health First Aid: Accredited training — schools pay per participant

Pattern: Schools are buying training (for staff/counsellors) and parent-facing materials (to hand to parents). This validates our Phase 2 direction.

## Key Themes and Learnings

1. Lived experience as founder story is a genuine differentiator — both YMHF and YoungMinds lead with it
2. Parent/professional/youth separation is important — different needs, different tone, different content
3. Free resource first, paid community/courses second — consistent pattern across best-in-class orgs
4. School licensing is proven — multiple orgs doing it successfully, validates our Phase 2
5. One-on-one coaching (ReachOut Parents model) is a revenue stream worth adding to our model
6. Certification/accreditation creates a reason to pay for training (MHFA model)
7. The NZ gap is real — none of these organisations serve NZ specifically
8. The Horizon Plan (YMHF) is the must-study comparator — closest to our vision, ahead of us on the journey
